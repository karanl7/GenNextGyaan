    Innovations from Ancient India

    Sanskrit Language

    SANSKRIT is an ancient language that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is considered to be the mother of all Indian languages and has played a significant role in shaping Indian culture, tradition, and literature.
    Sanskrit “has the largest body of literature in the world and has seen continuous production of literature in all fields of human endeavour”.

    Sushruta

    SUSHRUTA is considered the "Father of Plastic Surgery", and is responsible for the advancement of medicine in ancient India.
    Sushruta's theories about medicine, is supported not only by anatomical knowledge and surgical procedural descriptions contained within its pages, but by the creative approaches that still hold true today.

    Aryabhata

    In the 5th century, ARYABHATA introduced zero in the decimal number system and hence, introduced it in mathematics. After Aryabhatta, Brahmagupta described rules for zero in the 7th century.
    It is fascinating how the origin of zero bought changed and now it is used as a prime digit in mathematics.

    Baudhayana

    BAUDHAYANA is said to be the original Mathematician behind the Pythagoras theorem. Pythagoras theorem was indeed known much before Pythagoras, and it was Indians who discovered it at least 1000 years before Pythagoras was born !
    The credit for authoring the earliest Sulba Sutras goes to him.

    Srinivasa Ramanujan

    SRINIVASA RAMANUJAN FRS was an Indian mathematician. Though he had almost no formal training in pure mathematics, he made substantial contributions to mathematical analysis, number theory, infinite series, and continued fractions, including solutions to mathematical problems then considered unsolvable.

    Jainism

    JAINISM is one of the world's oldest religions, originating in India at least 2,500 years ago. Vardhamana Mahavira, the last Tirthankara was the founder of Jainism.
    The spiritual goal of Jainism is to become liberated from the endless cycle of rebirth and to achieve an all-knowing state called moksha.
